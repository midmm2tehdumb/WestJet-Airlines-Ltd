Article I — Identity and Structure

The official name of this organization shall be WestJet Airlines Ltd., hereafter referred to as “the Airline.” The Airline is headquartered in Calgary, Alberta, Canada, and shall operate as a professional virtual airline with structured management, operational divisions, and designated subsidiaries. The Airline shall operate all domestic, regional, and international flights under its main brand, with subsidiary branches operating under the direct oversight of central management.

The Airline’s organizational structure is hierarchical and clearly defined to ensure accountability, efficiency, and operational integrity. Authority is vested in the Chief Executive Officer (CEO), who shall serve as the ultimate governing authority of all airline operations, strategic decisions, and policy enforcement. Supporting the CEO is an Executive Team comprised of the Chief Operating Officer (COO), Head of Fleet and Routes, Human Resources Lead, Events Director, and other department heads as designated by the CEO. Each member of the Executive Team shall oversee their respective areas and ensure that operations comply with all airline policies and standards.

Below the Executive Team, Regional Managers shall be appointed for each major hub or operational region. Their responsibilities shall include supervising local operations, maintaining personnel compliance with airline policies, overseeing scheduling and routing, and ensuring that all flights adhere to established standards. All members, regardless of rank or division, are required to comply fully with directives issued by the Executive Team and the CEO.

The Airline shall maintain subsidiary divisions, including but not limited to regional and cargo branches. Subsidiaries shall operate in alignment with the policies, branding, and operational standards of the Airline. Each subsidiary shall have clearly defined operational limits, responsibilities, and reporting requirements to ensure integration into the overall organizational structure.

The Airline’s identity encompasses not only its operational role but also its professional reputation within the virtual aviation community. All members are expected to uphold this identity through adherence to professional conduct, operational excellence, and the promotion of a respectful and realistic flight environment. The Airline’s branding, communication, and operational representation shall reflect its identity and commitment to high standards at all times.

All organizational changes, including the addition of new subsidiaries, restructuring of divisions, or modification of operational authority, must be formally documented and approved by the CEO and the Executive Team. Such changes shall be communicated to all members in a timely and transparent manner, ensuring that all personnel remain informed and compliant with the latest operational structure.

Article V — Operations and Standards

WestJet Airlines Ltd. operates under a comprehensive framework of standards, procedures, and operational protocols to ensure safety, professionalism, and realism across all flights and airline activities. All operational staff, including pilots, crew, and ground personnel, are required to adhere strictly to these standards, which serve as the foundation for organizational consistency, safety, and credibility.

1. Flight Operations:

All flights must be conducted using approved aircraft types, consistent with the assigned route, passenger capacity, and operational purpose. Pilots must verify aircraft readiness, including fuel levels, maintenance status, and pre-flight checks, prior to departure. Flight plans must be submitted and approved according to the Airline’s procedural guidelines. Unauthorized deviation from approved flight plans is prohibited unless required for safety or emergency purposes.

2. Crew Responsibilities:

Flight crews must maintain clear communication at all times and operate in coordination with ground staff, air traffic control, and fellow crew members. Crew members are responsible for ensuring passenger safety, adherence to standard operating procedures, and timely completion of all flight tasks. Professional conduct, including courtesy, effective communication, and situational awareness, is mandatory throughout all phases of flight.

3. Scheduling and Attendance:

All operational staff must adhere to assigned schedules, including check-in times, flight assignments, and duty periods. Requests for schedule modifications must be submitted in advance and approved by supervisors. Absences or delays must be reported immediately to prevent disruption of operations.

4. Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs):

SOPs encompass all aspects of airline operations, including pre-flight preparation, taxiing, takeoff, cruising, approach, landing, and post-flight procedures. Pilots and crew are expected to follow SOPs precisely, ensuring compliance with safety regulations and internal policies. SOPs also include emergency procedures, communication protocols, and contingency planning for technical failures or adverse conditions.

5. Safety and Compliance:

Safety is paramount in all operations. Members must adhere to virtual aviation safety rules, maintain situational awareness, and report any incidents or irregularities promptly. Compliance with procedural standards, checklists, and operational directives is mandatory. Violations of safety protocols may result in disciplinary action, including suspension or revocation of operational privileges.

6. Reporting and Documentation:

All operational activities must be documented accurately, including flight logs, incident reports, and maintenance records. Reports must be submitted in a timely manner to supervisors or the Executive Team, ensuring accountability and traceability. Falsification, omission, or failure to report operational information is strictly prohibited.

7. Operational Ethics:

Members must uphold ethical standards in all operational activities, including fairness, integrity, and transparency. This includes avoiding conflicts of interest, reporting misconduct, and maintaining honesty in all communications and documentation. All decisions made during operations should prioritize safety, compliance, and organizational integrity.

8. Performance Standards:

Operational staff are expected to maintain proficiency, demonstrate competence, and continually improve their skills. Training programs, simulations, and refresher courses may be mandated to ensure adherence to operational standards. 9. Continuous Improvement:

The Airline encourages proactive engagement in improving operational practices. Members may propose enhancements to procedures, safety protocols, or training programs. All suggested improvements shall be reviewed by the Executive Team or relevant department heads, with approved changes implemented in a controlled and documented manner.

Failure to comply with these standards may result in progressive disciplinary measures, ranging from warnings and retraining to suspension or permanent removal from operational duties. The Airline reserves the right to enforce these standards consistently to preserve the safety, professionalism, and integrity of all operations.
